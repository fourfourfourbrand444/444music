FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about releasing music with 444Music.

How do I upload my music to 444Music Distribution?
+

Log in to your dashboard, create a new release, upload your audio file, cover art, and fill in the required details such as artist name, release date, and genre.

Which stores will my music be sent to?
+

Your music can be delivered to major streaming platforms including Spotify, Apple Music, TikTok, YouTube Music, Audiomack, Boomplay, and many more global stores — 100+ in total.

How long does it take for my release to go live?
+

Most releases are processed within 2–5 business days, but some platforms may take longer depending on their approval system. We recommend submitting at least 7 days before your target date.

What audio format should I upload?
+

We recommend uploading WAV files (16-bit or 24-bit) for best quality. FLAC is also accepted. We transcode to the required format for each store automatically.

How do I earn money from my music?
+

You earn royalties whenever people stream or download your music on supported platforms. Earnings are collected and shown in your dashboard — you keep 100% of what's yours.

When will I receive my royalties?
+

Streaming platforms usually report earnings every month, but some may take 2–3 months before the first royalties appear in your dashboard.

Can labels use 444Music Distribution?
+

Yes. Labels can manage multiple artists and releases from a single dashboard, with separate royalty tracking for each artist.

Is my music ownership protected?
+

Yes. You keep 100% ownership of your music. We only distribute your content to digital stores — we never claim any ownership or rights.

How does the referral program work?
+

Share your referral link with new artists. When they join and release music through 444Music, you earn referral rewards from their activity — passively.

Where can I find my referral link?
+

Your referral link is available inside your dashboard under the Referrals or Affiliate section. You can share it directly or copy it for social media.
