WHY WAS MY
RELEASE REJECTED?

We understand how frustrating this can be. The good news — most rejections follow clear store guidelines and can be fixed quickly. We're here to walk you through exactly what to do.

ℹ️
Important Notice

444Music does not randomly reject releases. All rejections are strictly based on content policies and technical guidelines enforced by stores like Spotify, Apple Music, Boomplay, and others. We are simply the messenger — and we're here to help you resolve it.

Common Reasons

WHAT WENT WRONG

01
©️
Copyright Infringement
What happened

Your release contains beats, samples, melodies, or instrumentals that belong to another artist or rights holder — and were used without proper authorization. Digital stores have automated detection systems (like ContentID) that flag this instantly.

How to fix it

Only upload content you have created, own, or have a valid license for. If you're using a purchased beat, ensure your license explicitly grants distribution rights.

Use original, licensed, or royalty-free content only
02
📝
Metadata Errors
What happened

Your release contains inconsistent or incorrect information — such as a misspelled artist name, wrong song title, mismatched album name, or metadata that contradicts the audio file itself. Stores require all details to be accurate and consistent.

How to fix it

Review every field carefully before submitting. Your artist name, song title, and release title must be spelled identically across all platforms and match the submitted audio exactly.

Double-check all metadata fields before resubmitting
03
🎚️
Low Audio Quality
What happened

The uploaded audio file does not meet the technical standards required by digital stores. This can include excessive background noise, distortion, clipping, poor mixing or mastering, or an unsupported file format (e.g. MP3 instead of WAV).

How to fix it

Ensure your tracks are professionally mixed and mastered. Export your audio as a WAV file at a minimum of 16-bit / 44.1kHz. Avoid over-compression and ensure there is no clipping in your master.

Upload WAV files — 16-bit / 44.1kHz minimum
04
🔁
Duplicate or Previously Released Content
What happened

This song has already been distributed to stores — either through another distributor, a different account, or a previous submission. Stores do not allow the same content to appear twice in their catalog under different accounts or listings.

How to fix it

If you previously released this through another platform, you must request a full takedown of that release before resubmitting through 444Music. Only submit fresh, unreleased material unless migrating from another distributor with proper removal documentation.

Remove existing release first, then resubmit
05
⚠️
Misleading or Fraudulent Content
What happened

Your release was flagged for using a fake artist name, impersonating an established artist, or containing misleading titles and descriptions designed to trick listeners or search algorithms. Stores take this very seriously and may permanently suspend accounts for repeat violations.

How to fix it

Always use your real artist name or your registered stage name. Never mimic another artist's branding. Titles must accurately reflect the content of the release.

Use accurate, truthful artist names and titles
06
🖼️
Artwork Does Not Meet Standards
What happened

Your cover artwork was rejected because it is blurry or low resolution, contains copyrighted images (e.g. photos taken from Google), includes social media handles, URLs, or store logos, or does not meet the required dimensions. Some stores also reject artwork with sexually explicit imagery.

How to fix it

Create or commission high-quality original artwork. It must be a perfect square at 3000×3000 pixels (minimum), in JPG or PNG format, with no text that matches the artist/title metadata exactly — and absolutely no third-party logos or social media links.

Minimum 3000×3000px, original artwork only
07
🔞
Explicit Content Mislabeling
What happened

Your release contains explicit language, profanity, or mature themes but was submitted without the correct explicit content label. Conversely, marking a completely clean track as explicit can also cause issues with store editorial placement and algorithmic recommendations.

How to fix it

Listen through your entire track before submitting. If it contains any profanity, sexual language, or mature themes — select "Yes" for explicit content in your submission. Clean versions must be entirely free of explicit content to be labeled as such.

Label explicit content correctly on every track

If your release falls under any of the reasons above, don't worry — simply correct the issue and resubmit your release. Our team reviews every submission carefully and we're always here to help you get your music live.
